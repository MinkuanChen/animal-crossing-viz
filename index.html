What are we looking at?

Per its Wikipedia page, Animal Crossing: New Horizons grossed $654 million in global digital sales by the end of 2020, making it the year's seventh highest-grossing digital premium game worldwide. As of March 31, 2021, the game has sold 32.63 million units. Because of the timing of the release around the start of the pandemic, the game not only was popular in terms of sales, but it allowed for a very interactive community of people stuck in their homes to come together from all over the world and help each other out in building great islands.

Explaining the community

When examining the community, it is important to not only understand when things are being said or how popular any one statement may be. It is important to look at the community as a whole. To do this, we tapped into the emoji use of the community to get a sense of what else is being communicated around the time of the big update. Emojis can reveal a lot about the users, from getting an overall sentiment/mood gauge regarding the game to what things people are adding to their island. In particular, the Animal Crossing community seems to like to use emojis to reflect current events in the game as well as other available game features such as flowers, plants, crop growing, and food.

Our team observed that the jack-o-lantern emoji was used most frequently, which aligns with our expectations as the tweets encompass the day of Halloween. We also noticed that for some reason, out of the two red heart emojis, one heart emoji was strongly favored in terms of overall usage, yet was not frequently used in the same tweet as other emojis. Yet the red heart emoji used less frequently was often paired with other emojis. We noticed that the coffee cup was a commonly used emoji, it seems that the community is very excited about Brewster's Cafe being added to the game through the November 4th update! What does the Twitter community for Animal Crossing look like

As you can see, the Animal Crossing community is still very active on social media platforms. As you may have gleaned from some of the visualizations above. There seemed to be a lot of activity starting on November 3rd and November 4th, prior to the stated release date. Well that is because it dropped early!
