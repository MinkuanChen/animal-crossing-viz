Animal Crossing: New Horizons

2.0 Update

Get Started

What are we looking at?

Per its Wikipedia page, Animal Crossing: New Horizons grossed $654 million in global digital sales by the end of 2020, making it the year's seventh highest-grossing digital premium game worldwide. As of March 31, 2021, the game has sold 32.63 million units. Because of the timing of the release around the start of the pandemic, the game not only was popular in terms of sales, but it allowed for a very interactive community of people stuck in their homes to come together from all over the world and help each other out in building great islands.

With the highly anticipated 2.0 update, we wanted to take a look to see if the already highly active community was reinvigoratted and also explore what they were talking most about!

Tweet Time Blocks Plot on the eve of November 4th and 5th, 2021

Here, we can look at the number of tweets that were sent out in 5 minute increments. We can see that overnight, there were many tweets sent out.

Top 10 Hashtags In the Animal Crossing Tweets from 10/30 to 11/05

Here, we can look at the number of specific hashtags used over a five-day span leading up to the release. It was interesting to see that there was a spike between Wednesday, November 3rd, and Thursday, November 4th, before a decline on the day of the supposed release. Was everyone too busy playing to tweet?!

Explaining the community

When examining the community, it is important to not only understand when things are being said or how popular any one statement may be. It is important to look at the community as a whole. To do this, we tapped into the emoji use of the community to get a sense of what else is being used to communicate around the time of the big update. (add insights)

What does the Twitter community for Animal Crossing look like

As you can see, the Animal Crossing community is still very active on social media platforms. As you may have gleaned from some of the visualizations above. There seemed to be a lot of activity starting on November 3rd and November 4th, prior to the stated release date. Well that is because it dropped early! According to Polygon and many of the Twitter users as well, the release surprisingly came out about a day earlier than expected.
